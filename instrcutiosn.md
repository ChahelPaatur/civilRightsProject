Here are 10 concise timeline events of significant African American civil liberty landmarks from 2010-2025:

2013 - Shelby County v. Holder (June 25, 2013) - Supreme Court struck down key Voting Rights Act provisions, weakening federal oversight of discriminatory voting laws.
2013 - Black Lives Matter Founded (July 2013) - Movement created after Trayvon Martin's killer was acquitted, becoming a major force against police brutality and systemic racism.
2014 - Ferguson Protests (August 2014) - Michael Brown's killing by police sparked sustained protests and national debate on policing in Black communities.
2015 - Charleston Church Shooting (June 17, 2015) - White supremacist killed nine Black parishioners at Emanuel AME Church, reigniting debates about racism and Confederate symbols.
2016 - Colin Kaepernick Kneels (August 2016) - NFL player began kneeling during the national anthem to protest police brutality, sparking nationwide controversy and athlete activism.
2020 - George Floyd Murder (May 25, 2020) - Floyd's murder by police officer Derek Chauvin sparked the largest civil rights protests in U.S. history.
2020 - Breonna Taylor Killing (March 13, 2020) - Taylor's death during a police raid became a rallying cry for police reform and the "Say Her Name" movement.
2021 - Derek Chauvin Convicted (April 20, 2021) - Former officer found guilty of murdering George Floyd, marking a rare police accountability victory.
2021 - Juneteenth Becomes Federal Holiday (June 17, 2021) - Congress passed and President Biden signed legislation making Juneteenth a federal holiday commemorating the end of slavery.
2023 - Affirmative Action Banned (June 29, 2023) - Supreme Court ruled race-conscious college admissions unconstitutional in Students for Fair Admissions v. Harvard, ending decades of affirmative action policy.



1. Brief description of your group (description of what rights and liberties have been denied)
African Americans have historically faced and continue to face systemic discrimination and denial of civil rights including voting restrictions, police brutality, unequal criminal justice outcomes, economic disparities, housing discrimination, educational inequities, and unequal access to healthcare. Despite constitutional protections, African Americans experience higher rates of police violence, mass incarceration, and barriers to voting through voter ID laws and polling place closures.
2. Identify some of the groups/organizations associated with the movement. What issues are those organizations and/or groups involved with?

Black Lives Matter (BLM) - Focuses on combating police brutality, systemic racism, and violence against Black communities through protest and advocacy.
NAACP (National Association for the Advancement of Colored People) - Works on voting rights, criminal justice reform, economic opportunity, and educational equity.
National Urban League - Addresses economic empowerment, education, housing, and healthcare disparities in African American communities.
Color of Change - Focuses on ending practices that unfairly target Black people through online campaigns and political advocacy.
Equal Justice Initiative - Works to end mass incarceration and racial injustice, particularly in the criminal justice system.

3. What are the current issues facing the demographic group?

Voting Rights: Voter suppression through strict ID laws, polling place closures, and restrictions on early voting and mail-in ballots disproportionately affect Black voters.
Police Brutality: Continued killings of unarmed Black individuals by law enforcement, lack of police accountability and meaningful reform.
Criminal Justice Inequality: Racial disparities in arrests, sentencing, and incarceration rates; mass incarceration affecting Black communities.
Economic Inequality: Wealth gap, lower homeownership rates, wage disparities, and higher unemployment rates compared to white Americans.
Healthcare Disparities: Higher rates of chronic illness, maternal mortality, and unequal access to quality healthcare.
Educational Inequity: Underfunded schools in predominantly Black neighborhoods, school-to-prison pipeline, and disparities in college access.
Affirmative Action Rollback: The 2023 Supreme Court decision banning race-conscious college admissions threatens diversity and opportunity.
